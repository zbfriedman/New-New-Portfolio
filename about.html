ZACH FRIEDMAN
I am a designer, creator, and innovator attending the Sam Fox School of Design & Visual Arts at Washington University in St. Louis, studying Design and Human-Computer Interaction.
I am a strong believer that design thinking methodology is the path to solving many individual and societal problems; designing beautiful, intuitive, and effective products begins with the insights of users.
Experience includes:
Design for America, Member
3D Print Westchester, Co-Founder
Creative Engineering, Engineering & Design Intern
Metalworks Inc., Design & Build Intern
Adaptive Design Association, Design & Build Intern
While attending Scarsdale High School, I co-founded 3D Print Westchester, an online 3D design and printing business where we created hundreds of unique items for a variety of clients spanning from New York to Texas to Australia. At the end of my senior year of high school, I worked with the parents, doctors and therapist of a 15-month old boy with microcephaly and cortical visual impairment to design and build custom adaptive furniture and devices that will grow with the child. This project was the culmination of extensive classroom learning and three hands-on internships. See this project page to learn more.
More recently, I have been altering my design education to be more UI/UX oriented.
